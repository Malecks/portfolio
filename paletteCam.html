Palette Cam

Create color palettes from your photos.

Available on the app store with over 250,000 downloads, and an average 4.6 rating

Project background

In January of 2016 I started a bootcamp for iOS development at Lighthouse Labs in Toronto. The 8 week program taught Objective-C, Swift, and the ins-and-outs of iOS development. As a final project we were tasked with creating a functional app to demo, which is when I came up for the idea of Palette Cam.

I wanted to build a tool for designers.

Project goals

Project Goals There were a number of goals I had for Palette Cam when I first started building that helped guide the development and product decisions throughout the process.

  • The app should be about utility

    Similar to a flashlight, calculator, or ruler app - I wanted Palette Cam to focus on a single promise and deliver it seamlessly without feature bloat.

  • Focus on design and ease of use

    I wanted to lean on my experience as a designer to help Palette Cam stand out from other colour apps and provide a bug-free, interaction-rich, joyful experience.

  • Be ready for the App Store

    This goal was maybe the most ambitious as it wasn’t a requirement for the Lighthouse Labs demo, but as a personal goal I had wanted to come out of my time in the bootcamp with an app that I could show friends and employers, ready to be downloaded to their own devices.

Technology used

For version 1.0 I relied solely on technology provided by Apple, and a single API from Flikr for the photo feed. Palettes were stored quickly in User Preferences, and I used standard UICollectionViews and other UIKit elements throughout to keep things lightweight and straightforward.

However as I continued to work on Palette Cam in my spare time, it became a testing ground for new libraries and technologies I was exploring as a developer.

  • IGListKit

    Provided easy to implement collection views, and auto-diffing with animations. I liked using this so much I ended up contributing a few lines to the open source project as well.

  • Realm

    A replacement for the quick and dirty solution of storing palettes in User Preferences. Realm provided an easy and free solution to real-time data storage.

  • Unsplash

    The inspiration page uses an infinitely scrolling feed of photos from the Unsplash API to ensure users can start with the best quality photos.

  • In-App Reviews

    The use of in-app reviews contributed to a massive influx of positive user feedback

Impact and Usage

Ever since launching Palette Cam I have been absolutely floored by the response and I’ve been especially amazed by all the ways people are using the app; from graphic design, to interior design, and even as a colour reference while painting. Palette cam has had great organic growth, and it has really encouraged me to keep working on and updating the project. Here are a few highlights:

  • Over 250,000 downloads on the app store
  • An average 4.6 star rating from over 1,200 reviews across all territories
  • Featured on the App Store in the 'Kick-Start your Brand(ing)' story
  • $0 spent on marketing or ads
